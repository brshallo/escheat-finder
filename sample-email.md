# Sample outreach email

This is a representative example of the courtesy note I send. Bracketed `{...}` items are filled in per record.

---

Hello {Name},

I found your contact information through {description of source for contact information} and wanted to send a brief courtesy note.

While searching California's public unclaimed property database, I came across a record that appears to be associated with your name:

{screenshot of the public record from claimit.ca.gov}

Please verify all details independently, as I may have reached the wrong person. You can search records directly at **claimit.ca.gov**

I am not requesting a share of any claim. I am reaching out as a private individual with no affiliation to any government agency or asset-recovery service. I am not offering filing assistance, legal advice, or representation. Claims can be filed free of charge through **claimit.ca.gov**, which also provides filing instructions.

If I have contacted the wrong person, or you are already pursuing a claim, please forgive the unsolicited outreach. If it turns out this message was at all helpful, I would welcome a reply letting me know.

Best regards,

Bryan Shalloway

P.s. One reason I decided to reach out is that many people are unaware these records exist, and property transferred to a state unclaimed property program often no longer benefits from future appreciation. NPR's Planet Money has a useful overview: https://www.npr.org/2025/03/19/1239428617/escheat-unclaimed-property-forgotten-money

If you're curious about how I identified potential matches from public records, you can find more information on the project at my github repo: https://github.com/brshallo/escheat-finder
